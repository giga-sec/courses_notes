Gathering to the Inbox

Taking control of your workspace begins with gathering.
Before we start, let me share a few best practices.
You'll put anything that's unprocessed into an inbox.

What does unprocessed mean?

Processed means that

  • you know what to do with it,
  • you've scheduled when to do it,
  • and it's where it belongs.
    So if any one of those three things is missing: what, when, or where; then an item is unprocessed

Large Temporary Box:

In the beginning everything unprocessed will go in these boxes.
Keep in mind that you don't need to gather things that are where they belong.
If something is in its correct home, it's where it belongs. And doesn't need to be gathered.
Think of a desk drawer that holds pens and nothing else.
Since it's a home for just pens you can leave them in there.
But any visiting sticky notes or other random objects need to go in the inbox.

One Week Inbox:

Also, one more temporary instruction. Label that special desk inbox one week.
After you complete your first gathering session, you'll take this label off.
Why? As you gather items, ask one question,
--> "Will I need this during the next week?"
And if so, put it in the one week box
rather than the other large boxes.

This temporary separation will let you build your time management systems without falling behind.
But beyond that one distinction,
Put absolutely anything else that's unprocessed into the other large boxes.
Don't throw it away; don't sort; don't organize. Just put unprocessed items into the boxes.

Let's reduce the number of physical gathering points in your office.
By now, you should

  • have several large empty boxes
  • your special desk inbox labeled One Week.
    We're going to put everything that's unprocessed in these boxes.
    Remember everything has a home and no visitors allowed?
    All random objects that aren't where they belong should go into the large inboxes.

Creation of Homes

Here's a good rule when creating homes.
Put the things you use the most closest to you.

  • Something you use multiple times a day, like your phone or headphones, might go on your desk.
  • Something you use maybe once a week, such as a stapler, can go in desk drawers.
  • something you use less than once a month in cabinets or out of the office.
    As you gather, try not to throw anything away, except for maybe food trash, like a candy wrapper.

Throw everything else in the big inbox.
Keep telling yourself, everything has a home, no visitors allowed.
Throw any item that violates that rule into the inbox.

If you still need to gather items from outside your office, what should you do?

  • Schedule an appropriate amount of time in your calendar to gather items from other locations.
    Yes. For larger areas, you will need about an hour per room.

Summary

In summary, as you gather,

  1. move all unprocessed items into one big inbox.
  2. put any items that need to be resolved or is usable for the next week into the temporary one-week inbox.
  3. don't throw anything away.
  4. gather anything visiting in the wrong home.
  5. dump any miscellaneous drawers or files into the inbox.
  6. create new homes as necessary.
  7. label homes as you create them.
